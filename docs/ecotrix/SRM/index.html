Definition of the Simple Regression Model

Overviewâ€‹

Premise: Analyzing the relationship between two variables, yyy and xxx, representing a population. The goal is to explain yyy in terms of xxx or study how yyy varies with changes in xxxâ€‹â€‹.

Examples: yyy as soybean crop yield with xxx as the amount of fertilizer, yyy as hourly wage with xxx as years of education, yyy as community crime rate with xxx as the number of police officers.

Model Formulationâ€‹

Issues to Address:

  1. Allowing for other factors to affect yyy apart from xxx.
  2. Determining the functional relationship between yyy and xxx.
  3. Ensuring a ceteris paribus (all else equal) relationship between yyy and xxxâ€‹â€‹.

  • yyy and xxx can have several interchangeable names:
    • yyy: Dependent, Explained, Response, Predicted variable, or Regressand.
    • xxx: Independent, Explanatory, Control, Predictor variable, or Regressorâ€‹â€‹.
  • uuu: Error term or disturbance, representing unobserved factors affecting yyyâ€‹â€‹.

Interpretation of the Modelâ€‹

Functional Relationship:

  • Linear effect of xxx on yyy when other factors in uuu are held fixedâ€‹â€‹.

Slope Parameter (b1)(b_1)(b1â€‹):

  • Represents the change in yyy per unit change in xxx, holding other factors fixedâ€‹â€‹.
  • Example: In a model where yyy is soybean yield and xxx is fertilizer, b1b_1b1â€‹â€‹ measures the effect of fertilizer on yield, holding other factors like land quality and rainfall constantâ€‹â€‹.

Limitation:

  • The linearity assumption implies a constant effect of xxx on yyy which might be unrealistic in many economic contextsâ€‹â€‹.
