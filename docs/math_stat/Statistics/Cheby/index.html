Chebyshev's Inequality

Definition​

For any random variable XXX with a finite mean μ\muμ and a finite non-zero variance σ2\sigma^2σ2, Chebyshev's Inequality states that for any real number k>0k > 0k>0,

P(∣X−μ∣≥kσ)≤1k2.P(|{X} - \mu|\geq k\sigma)\leq \frac{1}{k^2}.P(∣X−μ∣≥kσ)≤k21​.
